Ewedu Soup

Description

Ewedu soup is an indigenous yoruba soup with gbegiri(beans soup) used to supplement Obe ata(watery and peppery stew containing scotch bonnet pepper, bell pepper, chili pepper and a little tomatoes) majorly eaten with Amala

The above combination is a staple food among the among the Ibadan people

Ingredients

  • Fresh Ewedu leaves
  • Locust beans
  • Water

Steps

  1. Parboil the ewedu leaves with locust beans with little water.
  2. Place the parboiled content in a blender; and blend till a deep green viscous substance is formed.
  3. Put a pot on low heat and add the blended ewedu mixture for about 15 to 20 minutes; you can also add extra locust beans with a pinch of salt
  4. Turn off the heat your ewedu is ready to be eaten with gbegiri and Amala.
